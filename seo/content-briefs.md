# Phase 7 — Insight content briefs

Twelve briefs targeting long-tail local queries. Each is a complete editorial assignment: target keyword, outline, internal links, FAQ ideas, image direction.

**Author voice rule** — these posts should read like the rest of the SOHO site: editorial, unhurried, opinionated where warranted. Not SEO filler. No invented statistics. Where a claim needs a number (cost ranges, timelines), use the studio's actual experience; mark TODOs where data isn't known.

**Word count target:** 1,200+ words per post.
**H1 rule:** must contain the target keyword.
**Internal links rule:** every post links to (a) at least one service page, (b) at least one project, (c) the inquiries page.

## Publishing rhythm recommendation

- **Months 1–3 (foundation):** Posts 1, 4, 9 — the high-commercial-intent triplet. Cost, How-to-choose, Fees. These convert; they also build trust before the long-tail posts catch traffic.
- **Months 3–6 (depth):** Posts 2, 5, 6, 7 — the architectural-style/material posts. These are slower to rank but build E-E-A-T.
- **Months 6–9 (breadth):** Posts 3, 8, 10, 11, 12 — fills out the topic cluster.

Two posts a month is plenty for a small studio. One a month is sustainable. Don't bulk-publish — Google rewards consistency over a year more than 12 posts in week one.

## Images

Every post needs original photography. **No stock photos.** A second-rate post with the studio's own photos outranks a polished post with shutterstock, every time, on Google's quality signals. If a post can't get original imagery, push it to later.

## Internal linking discipline

Each post links to:
- One service page (anchors a money keyword to a money page)
- One project (provides proof, anchors topical authority)
- The inquiries page (conversion path)

Don't add more than 6–8 internal links total per post. Don't link the same target twice with different anchor text.
