# Keyword tracker

A simple monthly log. Fill in the rank for each keyword on the first of every month using Google Search Console's "Performance" tab (Average Position metric, filtered by query) or a paid tool like SE Ranking or Ahrefs Lite.

**How to read this:** lower is better. Rank 1 = #1 result. Rank 101+ effectively means "not ranking" — Google stops counting precise positions past page 10.

**How to use Search Console for this:**
1. https://search.google.com/search-console → Performance
2. Click **Date** → "Last 28 days"
3. Switch the "Average position" toggle ON
4. Click **+ New** → Query → Queries containing → paste the keyword
5. Read the **Average position** value from the right column

For paid tools, set location to **Calicut, India** specifically — rankings vary by geo, and the lazy default (national India) won't match what your prospects actually see.

**Cadence:** First of every month, fill the next column. Quarterly, review the trend column.

## Expected timeline (set expectations honestly)

| Window | What should be happening |
|---|---|
| Weeks 1–4 | Site indexed. Brand searches rank #1. Rankings still volatile, ignore the noise. |
| Months 2–3 | First movement on long-tails (residential / project-specific / blog terms). Brand-adjacent traffic grows. |
| Months 3–6 | Page 1 likely for at least one primary keyword. Top 3 depends on backlink velocity and GBP strength. |
| Months 6–12 | Top 3 for primary keywords achievable IF the off-site work (reviews, citations, backlinks, fresh content) is sustained. |

**Important caveat.** Ranking #1–3 for "architects in Calicut" is influenced by factors *outside* the codebase: domain age, review volume, backlink quality, competitor activity, and Google's local-pack algorithm (which weighs GBP signals heavily). The site work gets you *eligible* to rank. The off-site work (see `off-site-checklist.md`) earns the position.

If you're stuck at #5–8 after 6 months, the limiting factor is almost always one of:
- Not enough Google reviews (top 3 competitors typically have 30+ reviews to your 5)
- Too few quality backlinks (architectural press features)
- Inconsistent NAP across citations

Diagnose with: "site:competitor.com" search for them, vs. you. Look at their review count on Google Maps. Look at their press coverage. Match it, slowly.
